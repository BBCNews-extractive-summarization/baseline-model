Japanese shares have ended the year at their highest level since 13 July amidst hopes of an economic recovery during 2005. The optimism in the financial markets contrast sharply with pessimism in the Japanese business community. Despite this, traders expect strength in the global economy to benefit Japan, which has been close to sliding into recession in recent months. Structural reform within Japan and an anticipated end to the banking sector's bad debt problems should also help, they say.
