France's economic growth accelerated in the last three months of 2004, driven by consumer spending, a report shows. Consumer spending was up by 1.2% in the fourth quarter, and there also was a rebound in business investment that gave the recovery an extra shove. "France still has a strong economic growth," said Marc Toutai, an economist at Natexis Banques Populaires. "Consumer spending is still high. But French households have spent their savings to consume. "France can't sustain a high growth rate without an improvement in the job market. Despite the concerns, analysts pointed out that France was outperforming the majority of its European counterparts and that its economy was looking more robust than in previous years.
