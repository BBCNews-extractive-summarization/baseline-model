Microsoft is celebrating bumper sales of its Xbox sci-fi shooter, Halo 2. The game has sold more than five million copies worldwide since it went on sale in mid-November, the company said. Halo 2 has proved popular online, with gamers notching up a record 28 million hours playing the game on Xbox Live. According to Microsoft, nine out of 10 Xbox Live members have played the game for an average of 91 minutes per session. Halo 2 dropped one place to five, while Half-Life 2 fell to number nine. Record numbers of Warcraft fans are settling in the games online world. Nintendo has a 95% share of the handheld gaming market and said it expected to sell around five million of the DS by March 2005.
