American Olympic stars Justin Gatlin and Joanna Hayes have been named the winners of the 2004 Jesse Owens Awards, USA Track & Field's (USATF) top honour. Gatlin, 22, won the closest 100m final in Games history with a career-best time of 9.85 seconds in Athens. Hayes, 27, set an Olympic record of 12.37 in winning the 100m hurdles. "It is such a great honour to win the Jesse Owens Award," said Gatlin.
